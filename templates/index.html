WORKING

  • Voter Registration:
    Before an election, voters need to register to participate. This involves providing identification documents to verify eligibility.
  • Secure Authentication:
    Each registered voter is provided with unique login credentials or a digital ID, ensuring only eligible individuals can access the voting system.
  • Encrypted Voting Process:
    Voters log in to the secure voting platform using their credentials. They are presented with the ballot containing candidates or options relevant to their constituency or election. The voting system encrypts each vote, ensuring anonymity and preventing tampering during transmission.
  • End-to-End Encryption:
    Utilize end-to-end encryption protocols to safeguard the integrity and confidentiality of the voting process. Encrypt data at the voter's end, ensuring it remains encrypted throughout transmission and storage.
  • Tamper-Proof Infrastructure:
    Implement robust security measures to safeguard against hacking or unauthorized access to the voting infrastructure. Utilize blockchain technology or cryptographic techniques to create an immutable record of votes, ensuring transparency and auditability.
  • Verification Mechanism:
    Provide voters with a verification code or receipt after they cast their vote, allowing them to confirm that their vote was recorded accurately.
  • Auditing and Monitoring:
    Regularly audit the voting system for vulnerabilities and conduct security assessments to identify and mitigate potential risks. Monitor the system during elections to detect any unusual activity or attempted breaches in real-time.
  • Backup and Redundancy:
    Implement backup systems and redundancy measures to ensure continuity of the voting process in case of system failures or disruptions.
  • Independent Oversight:
    Engage independent third-party organizations or agencies to oversee the integrity and fairness of the voting process. Allow for independent audits and reviews of the voting system to ensure compliance with security standards and regulations.
  • Post-Election Verification:
    After the election, allow for independent verification of the results to ensure accuracy and fairness. Provide mechanisms for challenging election outcomes and resolving disputes transparently.
